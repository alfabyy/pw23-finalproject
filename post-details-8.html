China is again facing a surge in cases of respiratory pneumonia. In contrast to pneumonia in Covid-19, this disease is more common in children. Schools started to close. Hospitals in China are also starting to be overwhelmed by the surge in the number of pediatric patients. Schools were also closed.

So far, China claims that pneumonia is caused by bacterial infections, RSV, influenza and common cold viruses. However, seeing the spread among children, concerns arise that the severity of this disease tends to be overlooked. Meanwhile, Dr. Joseph Ambani, an infectious disease specialist, said that his party did not yet know clearly enough about this respiratory disease. "(Understanding) whether it is a new virus or a mutation of an existing virus is very important. This knowledge shapes our predictions about its spread and virulence. If it is a new virus, we will try to investigate it," he explained in Mirror News UK, Friday (1/ 12/2023). "The unknown, with the potential for higher virulence and lack of immunity in the population," he continued.

Not only in China, Europe is also experiencing the same thing. The Netherlands also reported a spike in pneumonia cases in children. Responding to this, Dr. Ambani admitted that there was a big challenge because the cause and spread of this disease were still not known with certainty. He stressed that the European health care system must prepare for the worst.
